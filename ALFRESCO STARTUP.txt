How to Block USB Ports Through Registry
Blocking a USB port through a registry can seem confusing. However, it is quite simple. All you have to do is follow the steps below:

Click on Start.
Now Click “Run”. Type “Run” in the search box if you can’t find it. Alternatively, you can press the ‘Window + R’ key to open the “Run” window.
Type ‘regedit.exe’ and click ‘Ok’.
This will open the Registry Editor. Here you’ll need to navigate to the following path: HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE>SYSTEM > CurrentControlSet > Services > USBSTOR.
Double-click on the “Start” option to open the “Value Data” box.
In “Value Data” enter 4 and click on “Ok”.
Close the registry and refresh or restart your desktop.
All your USB Ports will now be blocked.
Similarly, to open all USB ports, simply enter 3 in the “Value Data” box.
